Youth Unemployment Analysis

Data | Analysis | Visualizations | Notebooks | Report

Dataset Overview

This project uses the Youth Unemployment dataset, tracking the unemployment rate for individuals aged 15–24 over time, with additional socioeconomic indicators.

Key Features:

  • Year: Calendar year
  • Youth Unemployment Rate: Annual unemployment rate (%)
  • GDP Growth: Annual economic growth (%)
  • Education Enrollment: Youth education enrollment (%)

This dataset enables analysis of trends, correlations, and predictive modeling.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

EDA was conducted to identify patterns and relationships. Key insights:

  • Unemployment spikes during recessions (2008, 2020)
  • Negative correlation between GDP growth and youth unemployment
  • Higher education enrollment correlates with lower youth unemployment

Predictive Modeling

Models implemented to forecast youth unemployment:

  • Linear Regression: Captures linear trends over time
  • Random Forest Regressor: Captures nonlinear relationships, interpretable via feature importance
  • XGBoost Regressor: High-performance gradient boosting for time-series forecasting

Evaluation metrics included MAE, RMSE, and R² score. Random Forest achieved the best performance.
